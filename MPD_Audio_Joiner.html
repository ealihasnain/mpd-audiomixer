02 · PROCESSING
Pipeline options
Pipeline runs in dependency order: 80 Hz HPF → trim silence (per clip) → join → breath removal → auto boundary level match → de-esser → loudness → room-tone bed. HPF first so trim and level-match measure clean RMS; breath removal before level-match so stray breaths don't skew the RMS match; room-tone last so its level isn't squashed by the limiter. Defaults are tuned for ElevenLabs TTS — click any card to toggle.
Restores every toggle and slider to the ElevenLabs-optimised defaults.
Stage 2 · per clip
Trim silence
Removes leading and trailing silence from each clip before joining. Hysteresis-based detection preserves soft syllables.
dB
−50 dB = ElevenLabs-safe · −45 dB = conservative · −35 dB = aggressive (may clip breaths)
ms
Tail silence kept before hard cut — avoids abrupt endings
Stage 3 · concat strategy
Join with fade
Sequential concatenation — no overlap. Fades out the tail of each clip and fades in the head of the next. Words never mix. Zero = hard cut. Mutually exclusive with Auto boundary level match.
ms
0 ms = hard cut · 20–80 ms = clean voiceover join · 200–600 ms = noticeable dip · 1000+ ms = long fade
dB
Level the tail fades down to before the join. −80 dB = full silence · −20 dB = keeps room tone at 10%
dB
Level the head fades up from at the join. −80 dB = from silence · −20 dB = avoids hard onset, keeps 10% of signal
Stage 4 · joined buffer
Breath / click removal
Spectral-ratio detection: attenuates segments where low-frequency energy (80–400 Hz) dominates over mid (1–4 kHz). Runs before level match so stray breaths don't skew the RMS. Can eat soft consonants if too aggressive.
/ 10
1 = only obvious breaths · 5 = moderate · 10 = aggressive (DO NOT use above 5 without A/B check)
dB
How much to duck detected breaths. −18 dB is natural. −40 dB silences completely (often artificial)
Stage 5 · joined buffer
Auto boundary level match
Hard-cuts clips at the seam, then finds the last 2 spoken words from clip 1's tail and first 2 from clip 2's head. Measures their voiced RMS and smoothly ramps both toward a shared moderate level at the seam — noise floor excluded. Mutually exclusive with Join with fade.
ms
How far back/forward from the seam to look for spoken words. 1000–2000 ms covers roughly 2 syllables.
ms
Duration of the gain ramp applied at each side of the seam. 100–300 ms = transparent · 500+ ms = audible taper.
Stage 6 · joined buffer
De-esser
Reduces harsh sibilance ("s", "sh", "ts") common in AI-generated voiceover. Detects energy in the target frequency band and applies dynamic gain reduction only when it exceeds threshold.
Hz
6.5 kHz = ElevenLabs sweet spot · 6–7 kHz male · 7–8 kHz female / higher TTS voices
dB
Level above which gain reduction kicks in. −24 dB is a safe starting point for TTS.
dB
−3 to −6 dB = transparent · beyond −8 dB = audible lisp risk
Stage 7 · joined buffer
EBU R128 loudness
Gated ITU-R BS.1770-4 loudness normalization with K-weighted filters and absolute/relative gates. True-peak-limited output to prevent inter-sample clipping.
Stage 8 · final buffer
Room tone bed
Lays a low-level filtered noise bed under the entire mix. AI voice over perfect silence reads as synthetic; a faint bed restores the sense of a real space. Applied after loudness normalisation so it doesn't get limiter-squashed.
dB
−60 to −54 dB = subliminal · louder than −50 dB = audible hiss
Pink = warmest, most neutral · Brown = softer, warmer · White = brighter
